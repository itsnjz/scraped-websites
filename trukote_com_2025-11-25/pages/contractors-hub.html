PVC vs. TPO: Why PVC Leads

When comparing single-ply membranes, PVC consistently outperforms TPO in demanding commercial applications.

PVC Advantages

Long-term track record of 25+ years of performance.

More consistent Result when heat welded.

Proven resistance to oils, greases, and industrial chemicals.

Maintains flexibility and strength under thermal cycling.

Duro-Last PVC's non wicking scrim eliminates the need for edge sealing.

TPO Limitations

Less chemical resistance

Long-term performance data less conclusive.

Greater variability in seam weld strength.

At Tru-Kote, PVC is our standard specification for most commercial flat roofing projects.

Edge & Drainage Systems

Integrated Gutters

We install EXCEPTIONAL Metals integrated gutter systems, which provide:

Reduced chance of backup flowing into the eave

Better water management than standard external gutters.

Factory fabrication for precise fit and reduced field labor.

Full Coping vs 2-Piece Compression

Full Coping

Widely used, but introduce additional joints and fasteners.

2-Piece Compression metal Systems

Superior wind resistance, fewer leak points, much lower price point for the same coverage; often saving thousands of dollars compared to Coping.

Membrane Attachment Options

We provide multiple membrane attachment methods to meet job-specific requirements.

Adhered Systems

Smooth finish, fewer penetrations, higher wind uplift resistance.

Mechanically Fastened Systems

Cost-effective, faster installation, effective for reroof applications.

Induction Welded Systems

Fasteners are installed beneath the membrane, welded with induction tools. This reduces penetrations, distributes load evenly, and improves aesthetics.

Advanced Membrane Technologies

Kee (Ketone Ethylene Ester) Membranes

Kee membranes offer premium performance with:

Superior UV and chemical resistance.

Long-term flexibility in extreme weather.

Enhanced color stability.
